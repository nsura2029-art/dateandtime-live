12 · Tampa in context

Tampa sits on the western shore of Tampa Bay, a large natural harbor opening to the Gulf of Mexico. It's the third-largest city in Florida after Jacksonville and Miami, and the largest city on the Gulf Coast of Florida. The Tampa Bay metropolitan area is home to over 3.2 million people across Hillsborough, Pinellas, Pasco, and Hernando counties.

Founded as a military fort in 1824, Tampa grew wealthy on cigars and cattle before reinventing itself as a tech and finance hub. The city's Cuban-influenced Ybor City neighborhood was once called "the Cigar Capital of the World". Today, Tampa is home to the NFL's Buccaneers, MLB's Rays, and NHL's Lightning, all playing within a few miles of each other in the downtown waterfront district.
