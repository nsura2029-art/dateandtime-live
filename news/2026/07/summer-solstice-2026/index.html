Summer Solstice 2026: When Is the Longest Day of the Year?

📅 Published July 20, 2026 ⏱ 1 min read

The 2026 summer solstice falls on Sunday, June 21, at 04:24 UTC. It marks the longest day in the Northern Hemisphere and the start of astronomical summer.

Summer Solstice 2026

The summer solstice in 2026 occurs on Sunday, June 21, at 04:24 UTC. For the Northern Hemisphere, this is the longest day of the year — the sun reaches its highest point in the sky, and daylight stretches to its annual maximum.

What Is the Solstice?

The solstice happens when the Earth's axial tilt is most directly pointed toward the sun. For the Northern Hemisphere, this is around June 20–22 each year. The word "solstice" comes from Latin sol (sun) and sistere (to stand still) — because the sun's daily path appears to pause before reversing direction.

Cultural Significance

The summer solstice has been celebrated for thousands of years:

  • Stonehenge (UK) — Thousands gather to watch the sun rise over the Heel Stone
  • Midsummer (Scandinavia) — A national holiday in Finland and Sweden, with maypole dancing
  • Inti Raymi (Peru) — Incan festival of the sun
  • Kupala Night (Slavic) — Bonfires, flower wreaths, and folk traditions

When Is the Next Solstice?

The winter solstice in 2026 falls on Tuesday, December 22, at 01:50 UTC. After the summer solstice, days gradually shorten until the winter solstice, when the cycle reverses.
