1. Our accuracy commitment

We try to be as accurate as our sources allow. We use primary sources wherever possible (IANA, Nager.Date, Wikipedia, NOAA). We document our sources transparently. We fix errors quickly when reported.

That said, we are not an official time authority. For safety-critical timing (aviation, medical, legal, financial), please consult an official source (NIST, USNO, or your country's equivalent).

3. How we verify

Before publishing any new data, we cross-check against at least one additional source. For example:

  • Holidays: we cross-check Nager.Date against government websites for that country
  • Time zones: we compare our DST transitions against the actual tz database changes
  • On-this-day events: we verify each event against the linked Wikipedia article

We also run automated data quality checks against our database (e.g. cities with no time zone, countries with no capital, etc.). These run daily and surface any anomalies.

4. Update cadence

  • Time zone data: Updated within 7 days of any IANA release (typically 2–4 times per year)
  • Holiday data: Refreshed annually for the next 2 years
  • On-this-day events: Static curated list of 540 events, reviewed quarterly
  • City / country data: Re-imported from GeoNames ~annually

5. Error reporting

Found a wrong time, missing holiday, or bad data? Please report it:

  • Use the in-app feedback button (bottom-right of every page)
  • Email hello@dateandtime.live with subject "Data error"

We aim to acknowledge error reports within 3 business days and fix critical errors within 7 days.

6. Known limitations

  • Our climate data is a simplified lat/lon-based model, not actual historical weather data
  • Some countries have sub-national holidays (e.g. India has 28 state sets) that we don't yet cover
  • Multi-language holiday names are in the database but not yet displayed in the UI
  • Sunrise/sunset times are calculated, not measured — local terrain can cause deviations

We're working on all of these. See the homepage for our public roadmap.

7. Corrections policy

When we make a correction, we update the data in our database immediately. For significant corrections (e.g. a country was missing a major holiday), we may add a note to our changelog.

We do not maintain a public "corrections log" today, but each data row in our database has a "last updated" timestamp.
