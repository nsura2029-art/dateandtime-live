Frequently Asked Questions

What is a world clock?

A world clock is a clock that displays the current time in multiple cities or time zones simultaneously. It helps you track what time it is around the globe, useful for international calls, travel planning, market hours, and staying in touch with people in other countries.

How accurate is the world clock?

The world clock updates every second using your device's local time plus the IANA time zone database. Accuracy is typically within 1 second of official atomic time, depending on your device's clock synchronization.

Can I add my own cities to the world clock?

Yes. Open the Meeting Planner and use the search bar to find any of 33,945 cities worldwide. Your selected cities are saved in your browser's local storage and persist across visits.

Does the world clock show seconds?

Yes. Each city shows hours, minutes, and seconds (e.g. 12:34:56 PM), with a green pulsing dot indicating live data. The clock updates every second in real time.
