1. Our mission

Make it easy for anyone, anywhere, to know what time it is — and what comes next. We focus on the small details that big sites get wrong: the obscure city, the right holiday in the right country, the time zone abbreviation that actually means something.

2. Why we built this

The big time sites work fine for the 100 most famous cities. But if you live in, travel to, or work with people in any other city, you're stuck with broken search, missing data, or a clunky UX. We built dateandtime.live to fix that.

We cover 33,945 cities across 242 countries and territories. We support 408 IANA time zones. We surface 1,600+ public holidays and observances across 39 countries, with sub-national precision on the roadmap.

3. The team

We're a small distributed team of engineers, designers, and data nerds. We're not VC-funded; we just wanted this tool to exist and decided to build it.

If you spot a bug, have a feature request, or want to contribute data, please email us or use the in-app feedback button.

4. Data sources

We are transparent about where our data comes from. All sources are publicly available and properly attributed:

  • Time zones: IANA Time Zone Database (public domain)
  • City / place data: GeoNames (CC BY 4.0)
  • Country data: mledoze/countries (public domain)
  • Public holidays: Nager.Date + curated religion holidays from HebCal, Aladhan, and other sources
  • On-this-day events: Wikipedia (CC BY-SA)
  • Sunrise/sunset: NOAA Solar Calculator
  • Country flags: flagcdn.com (CC0 / public domain)

See our Editorial Policy for how we ensure accuracy.

5. Design principles

  • Speed first. The site loads in under 1 second on most networks.
  • Privacy by default. No third-party trackers. No cookies unless you opt in.
  • Mobile first. 70%+ of our traffic is on phones.
  • Server-rendered. Every page is fully readable before JavaScript runs, so it's fast, accessible, and SEO-friendly.
  • No dark patterns. We don't trick you. The cookie banner is honest. The "Accept all" button is the same size as the "Essential only" button.
