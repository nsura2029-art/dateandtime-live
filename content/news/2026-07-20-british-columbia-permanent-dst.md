# British Columbia Adopts Permanent DST

**British Columbia (BC)** has officially adopted **Permanent Daylight Saving Time (PDT)**. The one-hour clock change on **March 8, 2026** will be the province's final seasonal time shift, ending the practice of "falling back" each November.

## What Changed

Under the new law, British Columbia will remain on UTC−7 year-round (the same offset as PDT during summer). This puts BC in the same time zone as:

- Alberta (already on MST/UTC−7)
- Parts of eastern Oregon
- The Yukon Territory (which switched to permanentYST in 2020)

## Why the Switch?

Public opinion in BC has long favored ending the clock change. A 2019 survey conducted by the BC government found that **93% of respondents** wanted to stop switching clocks twice a year, with most preferring permanent daylight saving time over permanent standard time.

The main arguments cited by supporters:

1. **Health**: Reduces sleep disruption linked to spring-forward
2. **Safety**: Fewer car accidents during evening commutes in winter
3. **Economy**: More daylight for after-work shopping and recreation
4. **Alignment**: Matches Alberta, reducing business friction

## What About the US?

The US Senate passed the **Sunshine Protection Act** in 2022, which would make DST permanent nationwide. However, the bill has stalled in the House, and several states have expressed interest in following BC's lead.

States that have already adopted permanent DST (pending federal approval):

- **California** (Proposition 7, 2018)
- **Florida** (Sunshine Protection Act, 2018)
- **Washington** (2019)
- **Oregon** (2019)

## Practical Effects

For visitors and remote workers:

- **March–November**: No change. BC is on the same offset as before
- **November–March**: BC is now 1 hour **ahead** of Washington and California (instead of aligned)
- **Mexico and rest of Canada**: Time differences shift during winter months
