01 — The City

A 200-year-old port, reinvented

Founded in 1824 around Fort Brooke, Tampa grew into a cigar-rolling capital and cattle-shipping hub long before it was the Bucs' home and a tech corridor.

Today, the third-largest city in Florida is a quietly confident metro of half a million — known less for its beaches than for its reinvention. Ybor City's brick factories now host co-working spaces. Davis Islands still ferry commuters across the harbour, only now they go to hospitals and not cigar auctions.

Tampa's climate, for the 4,547th day running, is 87°F and sunny. It will still be 87°F tomorrow. This is not a complaint.
