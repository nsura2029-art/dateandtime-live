What is a time zone?

A time zone is a region where the same standard time is used. The world is divided into 40 offset zones (from UTC−12 to UTC+14) that span 26 hours, plus hundreds of regional variants (like America/New_York or Asia/Kolkata) that track DST rules for each area.

Daylight Saving Time (DST)

About 70 countries shift their clocks forward 1 hour in spring and back in autumn to extend evening daylight. The rules vary by country — the US and EU historically had different changeover dates, and the EU ended clock changes in 2026.

Coordinated Universal Time (UTC)

UTC is the world's time standard, maintained by atomic clocks. Every time zone is defined by its offset from UTC — e.g. UTC+5:30 for India, UTC-5 for New York in winter. GMT is an older name for nearly the same thing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many time zones are there in the world?

There are 40 named offset time zones (from UTC−12 to UTC+14) and approximately 408 IANA time zones when you account for regional variations. Each IANA zone (like America/New_York or Asia/Kolkata) tracks the full history of DST rules for that specific region.

What is UTC?

UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) is the world's primary time standard. It replaced GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) in 1960 and is maintained by a network of atomic clocks. All time zones are defined by their offset from UTC — e.g. UTC-5 for New York in winter, UTC+5:30 for India.

Why are some time zones offset by 30 or 45 minutes?

Most time zones are offset by whole hours from UTC, but several countries use 30-minute or 45-minute offsets for historical or political reasons. India is UTC+5:30, Iran is UTC+3:30, and Nepal is the only country at UTC+5:45. Parts of Australia also use UTC+9:30 and UTC+10:30.

What is the difference between GMT and UTC?

GMT (Greenwich Mean Time) is a time zone originally based on mean solar time at the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, London. UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) is a time standard based on atomic clocks. For most practical purposes, GMT and UTC are the same, but UTC is the official reference for all modern timekeeping, aviation, computing, and scientific work.

How does Daylight Saving Time (DST) work?

DST shifts clocks forward by 1 hour during warmer months so that evenings have more daylight. In 2026, the US will "spring forward" on March 8 and "fall back" on November 1. The EU ended DST clock changes in 2026 after a multi-year phase-out. Most of Africa and Asia do not observe DST at all.
